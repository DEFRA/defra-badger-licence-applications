<p><u>Veterinary support for badger vaccination&nbsp;</u></p>
<p>The Veterinary Surgery (Vaccination of Badgers Against Tuberculosis) Order 2010 permits people who are not veterinary surgeons to vaccinate badgers by injection against tuberculosis, subject to the conditions set out in the Order. These conditions include that the person administering the vaccine acts under the direction of a veterinary surgeon.&nbsp;&nbsp;</p>
<p>BadgerBCG is a Prescription Only Medicine-Veterinarian (POM-V). As such, it must be supplied in accordance with a prescription from a veterinary surgeon. Before prescribing BadgerBCG, the prescribing veterinary surgeon must be satisfied that the person who will use the product is competent to do so safely.&nbsp;&nbsp;</p>
<p>Condition 1 of the Licence to cage-trap badgers for bovine TB vaccination (CL48) requires that, before you set traps, you must have a veterinary surgeon available on call so they can be at the trapping location in less than an hour, if needed in an emergency.&nbsp;</p>
